Mary Mitchell Slessor

Famous Woman Rights Activist

1848 - Born in Aberdeen, Scotland.

1859 - Moved to Dundee, Scotland.

1876 - Set sail for Africa with Robert Mitchell Beedie.

1877 - She was assigned to Calabar as a missionary.

1879 - she contracted malaria and was rushed back to Scotland.

1881 - She had already returned and carried on her missionary work. She was visited by two deputies and they were impressed by her friendly nature with the locals.

1888 - She moved to Okoyong where she stayed in a place where other missionary had been killed for 15 years.

1892 - She became the Vice Consul in Okoyong where she was presiding over the local court.

1905 - She became the Vice President of Ikot Obong native court

1915 - She remained in Calabar till she died.
